Outreach

UC Berkeley's SWE outreach committee is committed to helping students develop a passion for science and engineering. We hold events for students in grades K through 12.

Mini University

Mini University is an event for Bay Area high schoolers to experience a day in the life of a Berkeley Engineering student. Participants explore careers in STEM fields through lab and campus tours, engineering activities, and student and industry panels.

Engineering Day

Engineering Day is a fun event in October where parents and K-3rd graders interact with Halloween-themed science booths in a casual, carnival style. In past years, we've had over 100 kids attend!

Read more
High School Engineering Program

HSEP is a 10-week long program that introduces female high school students to the principles and applications of engineering. Students participate engaging lessons and are mentored in small teams to carry out an involved engineering project.

Read more
SWE Science Saturdays

SWE Science Saturdays and SWE Science Scholars are monthly events for 4th-5th grade and middle school students. Students learn engineering principles by creating fun, hands-on projects.

Read more
Overnight Host Program

OHP gives women admitted to the College of Engineering and the Chemical Engineering major a chance to experience life as a Berkeley engineer. Admits tour the campus, interact with student organizations, attend a faculty banquet, and sleep overnight in student housing.
