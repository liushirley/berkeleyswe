Outreach

UC Berkeley's SWE Outreach committee is committed to helping students develop a passion for science and engineering. We hold events for students in grades K through 12.

Mini University

Mini University is an event that lets Bay Area high school students experience a day in the life of a Berkeley Engineering student. Mini-U exposes first-generation, low-income, and minority students to careers in STEM fields through lab and campus tours, engineering activities, and student and industry panels. For more information, see the 'Mini-University' tab under 'Outreach' or send an email to swe.miniu@gmail.com.

Engineering Day

Engineering Day is a fun event in October where parents are able to drop by with their K-3rd graders and interact with exciting Halloween-themed science booths in a casual, carnival style. In past years, we have had over 100 kids attend! For more information, contact swe.engineering.day@gmail.com.

High School Engineering Program

The High School Engineering Program is a 10-week long program designed to introduce female high school students to the principles and applications of engineering. Students receive engaging lessons pertaining to various fields of engineering and are mentored in small teams to design and carry out an involved engineering project.

SWE Science Saturdays

SWE Science Saturdays are a series of activities hosted once every month that teach engineering principles to 4th and 5th grade students. SWE Science Scholars is for 6th - 8th graders. Students work in groups to complete hands-on projects using their creativity and knowledge. Some previous activities include building catapults and “earthquake-proof” structures out of basic classroom materials.

Overnight Host Program (OHP)

The New Admit Overnight Host Program is a unique event for women admitted to the College of Engineering and the Chemical Engineering major to experience life as a Berkeley engineer. The program provides female admits the opportunity to visit the Cal campus, interact with engineering student organizations and research laboratories, attend a faculty banquet, and sleep overnight in student housing.
